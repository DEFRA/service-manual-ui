A small team ran a 12-week discovery from July to September 2025. They systematically used AI across every phase of the work.

## Key outcomes

The team achieved 56% faster user research synthesis. AI-assisted transcript summarisation and pattern identification made this possible.

A leaner team delivered at full quality. AI helped the team do more with fewer people without reducing the standard of outputs.

## Tools used

The team worked with ChatGPT, Gemini, NotebookLM, Claude, Copilot and Cursor. Different tools suited different tasks.

## How AI helped

AI supported the team across 6 areas:

- **Research synthesis** — summarising interview transcripts and identifying patterns across participants
- **Domain investigation** — quickly building understanding of complex policy areas
- **Document review** — analysing lengthy documents and extracting key points
- **Rapid prototyping** — generating prototype components and page layouts faster
- **Data collection** — structuring and processing research data
- **Codebase understanding** — navigating unfamiliar codebases during technical investigation

## Challenges

The team found several areas that needed careful attention.

**Ethics and bias needed continuous oversight.** AI outputs required constant review to catch biased language or assumptions. This could not be automated.

**Explainability was difficult.** It was hard to trace how AI reached specific conclusions, particularly in research synthesis.

**Multiple tools were complex to manage.** Switching between 6 tools created overhead. The team needed clear guidance on which tool to use for each task.

**The learning curve was steeper than expected.** Even experienced practitioners needed time to use AI effectively in a structured discovery context.
