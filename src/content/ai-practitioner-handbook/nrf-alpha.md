The NRF alpha ran from October 2025 to January 2026. It followed the AI-enabled discovery phase and earned one of the highest service standard assessment scores ever recorded for a Defra alpha.

## How AI was used

The team applied AI across 6 areas during the alpha:

- **Ideation** — generating and refining design concepts quickly
- **Research synthesis** — summarising findings from user research sessions
- **Document analysis** — reviewing policy documents and extracting requirements
- **Prototyping** — building and iterating on prototype components
- **Code exploration** — understanding existing codebases and technical constraints
- **Prompt library development** — creating reusable prompts for common team tasks

## Tools used

The team worked with ChatGPT, Gemini, NotebookLM, Claude, Copilot, Cursor and Obsidian. Each tool served a specific purpose in the workflow.

## Key outcomes

**Exceptional assessment score.** The service standard assessment recognised the quality of the team's outputs. AI helped the team produce thorough, well-evidenced work.

**Comprehensive prompt library.** The team built a library of tested prompts that other teams can reuse. This captures what worked and why.

**Lean team with full outputs.** A smaller team delivered the same quality and volume of work expected from a larger group.

## Lessons learned

**Prompt writing needs practice and documentation.** Good prompts take time to develop. Writing down what works helps the whole team improve.

**Validation must be embedded in workflows.** Checking AI outputs cannot be an afterthought. Build review steps into your process from the start.

**Adopt AI incrementally, starting with low-risk applications.** Begin with tasks where mistakes have limited impact. Build confidence before using AI for higher-stakes work.

**Always maintain human judgment.** AI supports decisions but does not make them. The team must own every output.

**Actively share learnings.** What one team discovers can save another team weeks of effort. Regular show-and-tells and written guides spread knowledge effectively.
