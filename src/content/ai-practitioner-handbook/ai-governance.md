Teams need a single source of truth for what AI tools they can use and what data those tools can access.

## What happened

Without clear governance, teams began using different AI tools based on individual preferences. Some used public tools that had not been assessed for security or data protection. Sensitive information was entered into services that did not meet government standards. There was no central record of which tools were in use or what data they had accessed.

## Why it matters

Ungoverned AI tool use creates shadow IT. This exposes the organisation to data breaches, compliance failures, and reputational risk. It also makes it harder to share learning between teams because everyone is using different tools in different ways.

## The lesson

Establish clear governance before scaling AI adoption:

- **Maintain a list of approved tools.** Make it easy to find and keep it up to date. See [approved tools](/ai-practitioner-handbook/approved-tools) for the current list.
- **Define data classification rules.** Be explicit about what types of data can be used with which tools. Make the rules simple enough that people follow them without thinking.
- **Create a tool approval process.** Give teams a clear route to request new tools. Assess each tool for security, data protection, and fitness for purpose.
- **Communicate regularly.** Governance only works if people know about it. Share updates through existing channels and make the rules visible in day-to-day workflows.

Governance should enable teams to move quickly and safely, not slow them down. If the process is too heavy, people will work around it.
