The most important thing when using AI at Defra is doing so safely and securely. Before exploring tools or learning resources, make sure you understand what is expected of you.

## Complete your mandatory training

The [AI Training and Guidance page on SharePoint](https://defra.sharepoint.com) is the best place to start. It includes Civil Service learning courses covering safe and responsible AI use.

All staff must complete the mandatory AI awareness training before using AI tools in their work.

## Understand AI governance at Defra

For AI policy and governance across Defra, visit the [AI Governance page on SharePoint](https://defra.sharepoint.com).

Before starting any AI-related initiative, you should understand:

- which AI tools are approved for use at Defra
- what data you can and cannot use with AI tools
- how to get approval for new AI tools or use cases
- your responsibilities when using AI-generated outputs

## Start with low-risk use cases

Teams that have successfully adopted AI at Defra started with low-risk, high-value tasks. Good starting points include:

- summarising meeting notes or documents
- drafting initial content for review by a subject matter expert
- generating test data or boilerplate code
- exploring and understanding complex policy documents

Always validate AI-generated outputs before using them in your work. AI tools can produce confident-sounding content that is factually wrong.
