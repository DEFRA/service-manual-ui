<h2 id="1-onboard-into-defra-if-youre-a-civil-servant-or-contingent-labour">1. Onboard into Defra if you’re a Civil Servant or Contingent Labour</h2>

### Meet your Line Manager or Resource Manager

Your Line Manager or Resource Manager (sometimes the same person) will be your main point of contact. They will:

- welcome you and explain how your team works
- answer any early questions
- help you set up your laptop

### Get an office introduction

Your Line Manager, Resource Manager or someone from your profession will show you around the office. They will:

- explain where your team works
- show you key areas like toilets, kitchens and emergency exits
- introduce you to colleagues and explain local office standards

### Get set up on SOP

SOP (Single Operating Platform) is Defra Group’s HR and finance system. You use it to:

- access your HR information
- complete timesheets and claim expenses
- update personal details
- get support from SSCL (SSCL manage SOP and support on HR and finances)

To set up your SOP access:

- create your SOP account as soon as your laptop is ready
- check you have DEF OTL self-service access (meaning you can complete timesheets)
- add your bank details (payroll cut-off is the 10th of each month)
- add your profession details:
  - First Profession: Digital, Data & Technology
  - Second Profession: User Research
  - Function: Digital, Data & Technology

If you need help with SOP or payroll, contact SSCL:

- Raise a service request on SOP
- Phone: 0345 603 7262
- Email: [Enquiries@gov.sscl.com](mailto:Enquiries@gov.sscl.com)

### Resolve any IT issues

You can call the Defra IT Service Desk on 0800 876 6458 (write this number down in case you can’t access your laptop).

You can also go to the MyPortal app on your laptop and then to the MyIT section of the app to report issues.

### Set up your email signature

Use the DDTS standard format:

First name Surname | Role Title | Digital Delivery | Digital, Data, Technology and Security | Department for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs | Contact number: XXX | Email: [name.surname@defra.gov.uk](mailto:name.surname@defra.gov.uk) | Office address: XXX

You can update this in Outlook via:

- New Outlook/Web: Settings → Accounts → Signatures
- Classic Outlook: File → Options → Mail → Signatures

### Set up your 1:1s and Objectives and get a Mentor

You must have at least one 1:1 with your line manager each month. Together you can agree how often you meet and for how long.

If you’re a permanent Civil Servant, you’ll also:

- set personal objectives with your line manager
- have a User Researcher mentor for profession-specific support

### Book travel

Book all work travel through Corporate Travel Management (CTM) using the CTM Lightning portal. Make sure you set up your CTM account first, check your details are correct, and read Defra’s Travel and subsistence policy pages so you know what you can book and any restrictions you must follow. Contact CTM’s support team if anything goes wrong with your booking.

### Claim your expenses

Claim your expenses in SOP using Internet Expenses. Read the Expense Policy first so you know what you can claim and the rates you must follow. Attach your receipts, submit your claim as soon as you can, and include all the required details. For help, use the step-by-step Expenses walkthroughs in MyHub.

### Complete your timesheets and use flexitime

Flexitime lets you vary your working hours day-to-day.

- read the flexitime policy and download templates from the flexitime and timesheet SharePoint site
- speak to your line manager before applying to change your normal working pattern
- share your flexi sheet with your manager every month

### Book annual leave

Use MyLeave (with your SOP credentials) to manage leave and absences. You can:

- check your leave balance
- add absences and amend requests
- read the MyLeave guidance

### Report absence

If you’re off sick or need unplanned leave, tell your line manager as soon as possible — and by 9am on the first day. Include:

- your reason for absence
- when you expect to return
- any urgent work that needs cover

### Complete your mandatory Civil Service Learning (CSL) training

Log in to Civil Service Learning to access and complete the mandatory courses. Aim to finish these in your first week. The health and safety training must be completed before you travel for work. The 4 modules are:

- Civil Service expectations
- Counter fraud, bribery and corruption
- Health and safety
- Security and data protection fundamentals

### Understand People Safety

Visit the Defra Health and Safety Hub to learn about safety policies and procedures. You can:

- report an incident using the form on the hub home page
- contact the People Safety Team at [peoplesafetyteam@defra.gov.uk](mailto:peoplesafetyteam@defra.gov.uk)

### Follow security and travel requirements

You must get approval from Defra Security before:

- travelling internationally for work
- taking Defra equipment overseas (including laptops, iPads, iPhones, official documents (including your pass) and any other Defra-issued devices)

You must also complete the Overseas Travel Form. Only use Defra-issued devices and software for official work. See the intranet for more guidance: Travelling and working overseas – guide.

<h2 id="2-onboard-into-defra-through-tpx-impact-if-youre-joining-as-resource-augmentation">2. Onboard into Defra through TPX Impact if you’re joining as Resource Augmentation</h2>

### Log in and confirm you’ve joined, and your IT kit is working

You’ll receive a ‘Welcome to Defra’ email from the UR Resource Managers. Reply to let them know you can log in and your IT kit is working.

If you have any access issues, email [User-ResearchResourceManagers@defra.gov.uk](mailto:User-ResearchResourceManagers@defra.gov.uk) and they’ll help you get set up.

### Introduce yourself to your Programme

Contact the programme contact named in your welcome email to introduce yourself. Let them know you’re online and available to attend any scheduled meetings.

### Resolve any IT issues

You can call the Defra IT Service Desk on 0800 876 6458 (write this number down in case you can’t access your laptop).

You can also go to the MyPortal app on your laptop and then to the MyIT section of the app to report issues.

### Set up your email signature

Use the DDTS standard format:

First name Surname | Role Title (Resource Augmentation – TPX) | Digital Delivery | Digital, Data, Technology and Security | Department for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs | Contact number: XXX | Email: [name.surname@defra.gov.uk](mailto:name.surname@defra.gov.uk)

You can update this in Outlook via:

- New Outlook/Web: Settings → Accounts → Signatures
- Classic Outlook: File → Options → Mail → Signatures

### Booking travel and claiming expenses

Read the Expense Policy first so you know what you can claim and the rates you must follow.

- before accruing expenses, get written approval from your programme
- book travel via the usual TPX route
- submit your expenses to TPX
- attach your receipts, submit your claim as soon as you can, and include all the required details

If you need help, email [User-ResearchResourceManagers@defra.gov.uk](mailto:User-ResearchResourceManagers@defra.gov.uk).

### Complete your timesheets

- complete your TPX timesheets as usual
- share timesheets, outlining days worked and hours worked per day, with your programme task manager and the resource managers at the end of each week

### Book annual leave

Tell your programme and your Defra task manager before booking in leave. Keep any leave trackers up to date.

### Report absence

If you’re off sick or need unplanned leave, tell your Defra task manager and TPX as soon as possible — and by 9am on the first day. Include:

- your reason for absence
- when you expect to return
- any urgent work that needs cover

### Understand People Safety

Visit the Defra Health and Safety Hub to learn about safety policies and procedures. You can:

- report an incident using the form on the hub home page
- contact the People Safety Team at [peoplesafetyteam@defra.gov.uk](mailto:peoplesafetyteam@defra.gov.uk)

### Follow security and travel requirements

You must get approval from Defra Security before:

- travelling internationally for work
- taking Defra equipment overseas (including laptops, iPads, iPhones, official documents (including your pass) and any other Defra-issued devices).

You must also complete the Overseas Travel Form. Only use Defra-issued devices and software for official work. See the intranet for more guidance: Travelling and Working Overseas- guide

### Ending your Defra placement

- Inform your Programme and Resource Manager of your intention to leave
- the UR Resource Managers will arrange the return of all IT equipment
- complete a handover that covers all outstanding actions and locations of all of your completed outputs
- make sure your final timesheets and expenses are approved and submitted
- return your Defra office pass if you have one

<h2 id="3-onboard-into-user-research-at-defra">3. Onboard into user research at Defra</h2>

User Research onboarding at Defra has three simple steps (listed below). Aim to complete these in your first week and before you start any research.

### Join the community

On your first day, email Research Operations [DDTSUserResearchOperations@defra.gov.uk](mailto:DDTSUserResearchOperations@defra.gov.uk) to let them know you have joined. They’ll connect you with the user research community and make sure you’re added to the right channels, including:

- **quarterly in-person user research community of practice** - a one-day, in-person event held in a central location or local hub focused on sharing work, learning from others, and connecting across teams
- **monthly online user research community of practice** - a monthly virtual 1-hour session with a Head of Profession update, learning opportunities update, celebrations, and a show-and-tell slot
- **User Research channels on Microsoft Teams** - once added, favourite the User Research community channels
- **the User Research community contact list**

### Read the standards, guidance and tools section of the UR manual

This section explains how we work as a profession — the standards we follow, how your work is assessed, and the tools available to support you. It also includes some mandatory ethics and safeguarding training (not applicable to supplier teams).

### Read and explore the research lifecycle section of the user research manual

This section explains how we plan, run and share user research at Defra. It includes our processes, step-by-step guidance and templates for each stage. You’ll also find information on the mandatory research planning process and how to share insights through the data and insight management process.

<h2 id="4-onboard-into-your-programme-at-defra">4. Onboard into your programme at Defra</h2>

If your programme has a Lead User Researcher, they will support your onboarding. If not, another team member, usually the Project Lead, Delivery Manager or another UCD practitioner, will help you get started.

With their support, aim to:

- understand the programme’s strategy, policy context, goals and roadmap
- meet your team and understand key roles and how you’ll work together
- join team ceremonies and embed yourself in the agile workflow
- get access to the work areas, communication channels and tools you need

If the team is not working in an Agile way, raise this with your Line Manager so they can help you establish suitable ways of working.
