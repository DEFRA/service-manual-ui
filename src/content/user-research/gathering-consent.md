## Why get informed consent

Informed consent ensures:

- participants decide voluntarily
- research is ethical and legal
- we meet MRS and GOV.UK standards

## What informed consent involves

You must explain:

- **voluntariness:** they can stop anytime
- **purpose:** who is doing it and why
- **activity:** what they will actually do
- **data:** what is collected and how it's handled
- **rights:** how to withdraw or complain

## Managing consent

<details class="govuk-details" data-module="govuk-details">
  <summary class="govuk-details__summary">
    <span class="govuk-details__summary-text">
      Withdrawing consent
    </span>
  </summary>
  <div class="govuk-details__text">
    <p>Participants can withdraw at any time, even years later.</p>
    <ul class="govuk-list govuk-list--bullet">
      <li>If they withdraw entirely: <strong>Delete all personally identifiable data.</strong></li>
      <li>If they allow existing data: <strong>Anonymise retained data.</strong></li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</details>

<details class="govuk-details" data-module="govuk-details">
  <summary class="govuk-details__summary">
    <span class="govuk-details__summary-text">
       Recordings and observers
    </span>
  </summary>
  <div class="govuk-details__text">
    <p>You must tell participants about any recordings or observers.</p>
    <p><strong>Maximise participation:</strong> Offer the option to take part without being recorded.</p>
  </div>
</details>

### Process

1.  **Tailor the form**: Edit only text in `< >` brackets.
2.  **Send ahead**: Give participants time to read it before the session.
3.  **Store records**: Keep completed forms in the UR Vault.
