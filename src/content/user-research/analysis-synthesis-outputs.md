Defra user researchers adopt a wide range of analysis and synthesis techniques and create a range of research outputs. Guidance and templates are listed below.

## Preparing observers and note-takers

- Arrange observers and note-takers
- Observation and note-taking guidance
- Observation and note-taking templates

## Conducting analysis and synthesis

- Team workshops
- Thematic analysis
- Affinity mapping
- User journey mapping
- Behaviour observation (rainbow spreadsheets)
- Survey data preparation
- Service desk preparation
- Prioritisation techniques

## Research outputs

- User groups and personas
- Empathy maps
- User needs
- User journey maps
- Video and image outputs
- General report templates
- Design stage report templates (Discovery, Alpha, Beta, Live)
- Service assessment report templates (Discovery, Alpha, Beta, Live)

## Standards

All analysis, synthesis and outputs must comply with Defra’s user research standards and guidance.
