# NRF discovery: AI-accelerated user research

A 12-week discovery completed in September 2025. The team used AI throughout and delivered comprehensive results.

## Overview

This discovery ran from July to September 2025 with a lean team. The team made a deliberate decision to use AI tools extensively, not just in isolated areas.

They delivered a complete discovery within the standard 12-week timeframe. All expected outputs met a high standard. This proved that AI could accelerate delivery work without compromising quality.

The team's approach was pragmatic. They experimented with different tools for different tasks, learned what worked and what did not, and adjusted their methods throughout the project.

## How AI supported the work

**User research synthesis** became significantly faster. AI helped summarise interview transcripts and identify patterns across multiple sessions, cutting synthesis time by 56%.

**Research into technical and policy domains** meant the team could investigate areas outside their immediate expertise. They did not need to hire additional specialists for every question.

**Document analysis** sped up dramatically. The team used AI to review existing documentation, extract relevant sections, and identify gaps or inconsistencies.

**Prototyping** allowed the team to test concepts with users quickly. AI generated working code, letting the team focus on design and user needs rather than technical implementation.

**Website scrapers** gathered data from public sources. This automated work that would otherwise have required manual collection.

**Code exploration** helped the team understand unfamiliar codebases and technical systems faster.

## Tools used

- OpenAI ChatGPT
- Google Gemini
- Google NotebookLM
- Anthropic Claude
- Microsoft Copilot
- Cursor

## Outcomes and impact

### 56% faster user research synthesis

User research completion and synthesis took 56% less time than traditional methods. Insights reached decision-makers faster.

### A lean team delivered full outputs

The team operated with fewer people while maintaining the same output quality. This efficiency came from AI handling time-consuming tasks like transcription, initial analysis, and document review.

### Better collaboration across stakeholders

AI tools helped create clearer documentation and summaries. Better information sharing led to better alignment between the delivery team and the wider programme.

### Quality matched standard discovery expectations

The discovery received positive feedback at its assessment. The quality of outputs matched or exceeded expectations for a standard discovery.

## Challenges and what the team learned

**Ethics and bias** required constant attention. The team established validation steps to check AI outputs for bias or inaccuracies before using them in decision-making.

**Explainability** presented challenges when AI produced useful outputs without making clear how it reached conclusions. The team learned to assess reasoning carefully rather than accepting outputs at face value.

**Technical complexity** increased with multiple tools. Maintaining and troubleshooting AI tools added overhead to workflows. The team learned to verify outputs carefully and use AI as a starting point rather than a final answer.

**The learning curve** was steeper than expected. Team members needed time to learn effective prompt writing and recognise unreliable outputs. This required upfront investment, but as skills improved, efficiency gains accelerated.
