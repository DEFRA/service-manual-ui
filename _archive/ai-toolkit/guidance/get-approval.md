<p class="govuk-body-l">Before you build an AI feature or service, check what approvals you need. Some are specific to AI. Some apply to any digital spend. Sorting them early saves rework later.</p>

Talk to AICE before you start. We can help you work out which of these apply and point you to the right route.

## Check if your work needs spend approval

AI work goes through the same digital and technology spend controls as any other build. Sign-off from an assurance board is needed above set thresholds, currently around £100,000 for anything public-facing and £1 million for everything else. Larger programmes need a full business case.

HM Treasury approval is needed for spend that is **novel**, and using AI where your team has no internal expertise can count as novel. Check the current thresholds and routes in the [digital and technology spend controls](https://www.gov.uk/government/collections/cabinet-office-controls) before you commit budget.

## Do a DPIA if you touch personal data

If your service will process personal data, a Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) is required before any processing begins. Treat it as a near-certainty for AI, because AI usually meets the criteria that make a DPIA mandatory. See [Keeping data safe](/ai-toolkit/guidance/keeping-data-safe).

## Check if you need an ATRS record

If your tool affects the public, you may need to publish a record in the Algorithmic Transparency Recording Standard. [Ethics](/ai-toolkit/guidance/ethics) explains the trigger and when it applies.

## Plan for your service assessment

An AI service still faces a normal [service assessment](/service-assessments) against the Service Standard, and passing is a condition of spend approval. Bring your evaluation evidence, your ATRS status and your DPIA to the panel. See [Test and assure your AI service](/ai-toolkit/guidance/test-and-assure) for what to prepare.

## Your pre-build checklist

<ul class="govuk-list govuk-list--bullet govuk-list--spaced">
<li>spend control sign-off, if you are over the threshold</li>
<li>HM Treasury approval, if the work is novel</li>
<li>a DPIA, if you process personal data</li>
<li>an ATRS record, if the tool affects the public</li>
<li>a plan for your service assessment</li>
</ul>

If any of these is unclear, talk to AICE before you write code.
