AI is a collaborator, not a replacement. Treat it as a capable assistant that needs clear direction and careful oversight.

## Break problems into smaller parts

Large, open-ended prompts produce unreliable results. Decompose complex tasks into focused steps. Each step should have a clear input and expected output. This makes the AI's work easier to verify and correct.

## Every output needs human review

AI can produce confident-sounding results that are wrong. Always check generated code, text, and data before using it. Review for correctness, security, and alignment with your requirements.

## Build generalist skills

Teams working with AI benefit from broad technical knowledge. Understanding architecture, testing, security, and operations helps you guide the AI effectively and spot problems in its output.

## Craft detailed prompts

Invest time in writing good prompts. Specify the context, constraints, format, and expected behaviour. Detailed prompts produce better results and reduce the need for follow-up iterations. See [Writing good prompts](/ai-toolkit/guidance/writing-good-prompts).

## Test ideas quickly

AI lets you prototype and validate ideas faster than before. Use this speed to explore options early and discard approaches that do not work. Fail fast and learn.

## Watch for cognitive strain

Working with AI can be intense. Constant rapid iteration is tiring. Pair on hard problems, take structured breaks, and step away from the screen when you need to think. The team that runs cleaner, slower cycles often produces better work than one that hammers prompts non-stop.
